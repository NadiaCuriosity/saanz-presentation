Summary

Most of us are using AI as a smart Google: type a question, get an answer, copy-paste it somewhere. That's the least exciting thing AI can do. The gap between what AI promises and what we've experienced isn't a technology problem, it's a briefing problem. We haven't told AI who we are, what our mountain is about, or what we need.


The session walked through a four-level AI spectrum (Ask & Answer → AI Teammate → AI Workflow → AI Agent), a practical prompting framework (YETI), and a live demo showing how voice-first interaction produces dramatically better results. For ski area operators who spend their days on the mountain rather than at a desk, talking to AI is the single biggest unlock.


The core message: AI, applied well, doesn't replace the people who create the magic on the mountain. It protects them by stripping away repetitive work so they have the headspace to show up as their best selves.

Glossary

LLM (Large Language Model)
The technology behind tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Trained on vast amounts of text to understand and generate language. Each update is called a new "model."
Context Window
How much information AI can hold in working memory at once. When you exceed it, the AI starts forgetting earlier parts of the conversation. Think of it like a meeting that's gone too long.
Thread
A single conversation with AI. Best practice: one topic per thread.
Custom GPT / Project / Gem / Skills
A saved set of instructions and knowledge files that persist across conversations. Think of it as onboarding your AI with a job description and reference materials so you don't have to repeat yourself.
Prompt
Any input you give to AI, whether typed, spoken, or uploaded as a file.
AI Slop
Generic, low-quality AI output. Usually caused by giving too little context or briefing.
Sycophancy
AI's tendency to agree with you and tell you you're amazing. Important to be aware of, especially when using AI as a thinking partner.
Training Data
The information AI was trained on, accurate up to a specific date. For current events, AI needs to use its web search tool.

Key Concepts

The AI Spectrum

What it is

Four levels of AI use, from basic to autonomous.


Why it matters

Knowing where you are helps you see the next step. Most of us are at level one.


Key takeaway

Moving from Ask & Answer to AI Teammate (giving AI context about your business) is the biggest value jump. 67% of survey respondents hadn't done this yet.

LevelWhat it looks like
Ask & AnswerType a question, get a response, copy-paste it somewhere
AI TeammateAI knows your business, voice, and processes. Works with context
AI WorkflowTriggered actions that happen without you prompting
AI AgentYou give it a task. It decides how to do it and tells you when it's done

The Digital Teammate

What it is

AI is closer to working with a person than operating a spreadsheet. Treat it like a brilliant new seasonal hire: smart, eager, knows nothing about your mountain.


Why it matters

If you give AI no context, you get the most generic, probable answer. Just like a new hire with no onboarding.


Key takeaway

Brief your AI with context (who you are, what your mountain is about), a job description (how to behave, what to avoid), knowledge (SOPs, FAQs, brand guide), and one critical instruction: "If you're not sure, say so. Don't guess."

The YETI Framework

What it is

A simple framework for getting quality AI outputs, especially using voice.


Why it matters

Works in under 60 seconds. Eliminates the blank prompt problem.


Key takeaway

Use this every time you need AI to do something meaningful.

StepWhat to do
YarnGive the full picture. Context, who you are, what the problem is, who it's for
ExpertiseTell it who to be. Data scientist, Kim Scott, organisational psychologist
TaskWhat you want it to produce. Be specific about format and stakes
Interview"Ask me up to three questions, one at a time, to get the context you need"

Voice-First AI

What it is

Stop typing, start talking. AI understands natural speech and is excellent at taking unstructured verbal input and organising it.


Why it matters

Ski area operators aren't at desks. You're on the mountain, in the rental shop, walking between buildings. Voice input matches your work reality.


Key takeaway

Hit the microphone button every time. Brain dump while you're moving. Use it for hazard reports, meeting notes, email drafts, brain dumps.

Own the Playbook, Rent the Tech

What it is

Your processes, knowledge, brand voice, and operating models are the asset. The AI platform is just the delivery mechanism.


Why it matters

Platforms change constantly. If your knowledge is documented and portable, you can move it anywhere.


Key takeaway

Don't get locked into one platform. Invest in documenting your processes and knowledge, not learning one tool's interface.

Meeting Intelligence

What it is

Record a meeting → AI transcribes it → AI identifies what's relevant to each department → tailored summaries routed to the right channel automatically.


Why it matters

No one should sit through a 90-minute recap to find the two things that matter to them.


Key takeaway

This is achievable with current tools. The prerequisite is documenting what each department needs from meetings. See the diagram →

Questions Raised

Do you have to type, or can you scan/copy/drag files in?
All of the above. Drag files, talk to it, take photos, paste screen grabs. AI can see images, hear voice, and process documents.
Does AI remember what you've told it?
Don't rely on memory across sessions, but it is getting better. If it's a recurring task, document your context in a Custom GPT or Project. One-off tasks don't need this.
How do you move from basic use to automation (triggers, folders)?
Automation platforms exist for this. The foundation is still good prompting and documenting your process step by step. You have to get the process out of your head first. See platform recommendations above.
Is it worth paying for ChatGPT?
Yes. Free versions use older, less capable models. ChatGPT Plus (~$40 NZD/month) unlocks smarter models, voice mode, Custom GPTs, and file uploads.
Does mixing and matching AI platforms matter?
Not at all. The principles transfer between platforms. They all have their own quirks, but the fundamentals are the same. Own the playbook, rent the tech.
How do we set up multiple users?
Options: you can share a ChatGPT login to start (everyone sees everything), separate paid accounts for privacy, or use Gemini free via Google Workspace. Watch file permissions if using Microsoft Copilot. All the platforms have options for teams and workplaces.
What about environmental impact?
There's no universal metric for AI's environmental cost. Water usage stats are unreliable. Data centres (AI + internet) currently use 1.5-3% of global power. It's a legitimate concern, but context matters. For perspective: dairy production is more resource-intensive per unit than an AI prompt.
What about data security and privacy?
Find your settings and turn off "improve the model for everybody." On paid plans, your data is not used for training by default. The larger security risk is hackers using AI to exploit systems, not the models themselves leaking your data. Be vigilant if you are using Claude Code or anything in the CLI. Downloading skills, packages, extensions from GitHub/VS Code and similar can be risky.

Try This Next

1

Pay for it.

Free models are why you think AI is average. The paid version is a completely different experience.

2

Talk to it.

Open ChatGPT, hit the voice button, and brain dump about your mountain for two minutes. Then say: "Do you have any questions for me before we proceed?" Watch what happens.

3

Brief your first AI teammate.

Pick one thing you do more than three times a week (incident reports, staff FAQ, meeting notes). Spend 30 minutes setting up a Custom GPT / Microsoft Agent / Claude Project / or Google Gem with your context and instructions. Refine it over a week. You can't break anything.

4

Turn off the training toggle.

Open ChatGPT (or whichever platform you use). Go to Settings → Data Controls → turn off "Improve the model for everyone."

5

Try the YETI framework.

Next time you need AI to help with something, use voice and walk through Yarn → Expertise → Task → Interview. Notice the difference in output quality.
